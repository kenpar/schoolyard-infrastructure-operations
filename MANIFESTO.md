# The Schoolyard Operations Ecosystem Manifesto

## Laying the First Stone

Every lasting structure begins with a single foundation stone.

This document represents that foundation.

The Schoolyard Operations Ecosystem is not being created to document technology. It is being created to preserve operational knowledge.

Technology changes.

Servers are replaced.

Applications evolve.

Cloud providers come and go.

People move on.

Knowledge must endure.

The purpose of this ecosystem is to ensure that the understanding required to operate, maintain, recover and evolve the business never depends upon a single individual.

It belongs to the organisation.

Today marks the deliberate beginning of that journey.

Everything that follows will be built upon the principles defined within this manifesto.

---

# Our Belief

We believe that technology should be understandable.

Every business depends upon technology, yet very few business owners truly understand the services that support their organisation.

Most documentation is written for engineers.

We believe documentation should first be written for the business, then progressively reveal deeper technical knowledge as required.

Understanding should never be limited by technical expertise.

Every person interacting with a business service should be able to understand it from the perspective most relevant to their responsibilities.

---

# Our Vision

To build a living operational ecosystem that preserves knowledge rather than simply recording information.

To create a single source of operational truth that can be understood by business owners, operational managers and engineers alike.

To ensure that every business service can be understood through multiple views without fragmenting its knowledge into separate documents.

One service.

One source of truth.

Multiple views.

One shared understanding.

---

# Our Promise

Every service documented within this ecosystem shall answer three fundamental questions.

Why does this service exist?

Where does this service live?

How does this service work?

From these questions, every audience can progressively explore deeper knowledge without losing sight of the business purpose that the technology exists to serve.

Technology exists to enable the business.

The Operations Ecosystem exists to make that relationship visible.

## Project Delivery Principle

A technology project shall not be considered complete when the software is deployed.

A project is complete only when the business service it creates is understood, documented, recoverable, supportable and capable of being handed over.

Every future project shall include an Operational Knowledge Model that explains the service from business, operational and engineering perspectives.
